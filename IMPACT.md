# Eve Os: Triage — Field Medical Coordination That Works Anywhere

> A complete, free, offline-capable medical coordination system designed for conflict zones, disaster response, and resource-limited environments. No internet required. No subscription. No servers. Just a laptop and the people who need it.

---

## The Problem

In active conflict zones and disaster areas, medical teams face a brutal reality:

- **No internet** — cell towers are down, infrastructure is destroyed
- **No shared language** — patients, medics, and translators speak different languages, and miscommunication costs lives
- **No records** — paper charts get lost, wet, burned; handoff between shifts is verbal and error-prone
- **No coordination** — who is where, what supplies are left, which patients are critical — nobody has the full picture
- **No time** — triage decisions happen in seconds, not minutes

Every one of these problems has been solved here.

---

## What This System Does

### 🏥 Patient Intake in Under 60 Seconds
A guided, 6-step intake process walks any medic — trained or volunteer — through capturing a patient's demographics, injuries, vitals, and clinical findings. The system then **automatically generates a treatment plan** based on military MARCH protocol (Massive hemorrhage, Airway, Respiration, Circulation, Hypothermia).

No medical AI experience required. The system does the clinical thinking.

### 🗺️ Live Ward Map
A visual, real-time map of every patient in every room across every ward. Color-coded by severity. Click any patient to see their full record, treatment plan, vitals history, and medication schedule. Move patients between rooms with drag-and-drop.

Field hospitals can see their entire operation at a glance.

### 🌍 42 Languages — Instantly
The entire interface — every button, label, clinical term, treatment instruction — works in **42 languages**, including:

Arabic, Farsi, Pashto, Kurdish, Ukrainian, Russian, Amharic, Somali, Swahili, Hausa, Yoruba, French, Spanish, Chinese, Hindi, Bengali, Burmese, Khmer, Thai, Vietnamese, and many more.

Switch languages with a single dropdown. The treatment plan regenerates in the new language instantly. No delay. No internet connection needed for the static interface.

### 🗣️ Voice That Speaks Every Language
The system includes a text-to-speech engine that can **speak in all 42 languages** — even languages the underlying model was never trained on. This was achieved through a novel approach: breaking generated text into phonemes (sound units) and reconstructing speech from them.

A medic who speaks only English can have the system speak instructions to a patient in Pashto, Arabic, or Burmese.

### 💬 Real-Time Communication
- **Video calls** between medics across the network — no internet, just local mesh
- **Text messaging** with automatic translation
- **Voice notes** that can be transcribed and translated

A surgeon at a base hospital can video-call a field medic and see the patient in real time.

### 🤖 Medical AI Assistant
An integrated medical AI (powered by Google's MedGemma) that medics can consult for:
- Differential diagnosis suggestions
- Drug interaction warnings  
- Treatment protocol guidance
- Clinical decision support

The AI runs **locally on the device** — no cloud, no data leaving the machine, no privacy concerns.

### 📦 Supply & Inventory Tracking
Real-time inventory of medications, equipment, and supplies across all locations. Automatic warnings when stock runs low. Tracks consumption patterns so resupply can be anticipated before it's needed.

### 📋 Patient Records & Export
Complete digital medical records that can be:
- Exported as printable documents in any language
- Handed off cleanly between shifts
- Archived when patients are discharged
- Carried on a USB drive if the team needs to relocate

### ✈️ Works Completely Offline
The entire system — AI, translation, voice, records, everything — runs on a single laptop with no internet connection. It's designed for:
- Field hospitals with no infrastructure
- Mobile medical units
- Refugee camps
- Areas under active bombardment where connectivity is impossible

## Why This Matters Right Now

### Active Conflicts
In Yemen, Sudan, Gaza, Ukraine, Myanmar, and the DRC, medical infrastructure is being destroyed faster than it can be rebuilt. Medical teams are working with paper, whiteboards, and shouted handoffs. Language barriers between international medical volunteers and local patients are costing lives daily.

### The Language Gap Kills
A medic from France treating a patient in Sudan who speaks Arabic and Hausa has no way to explain a treatment plan, ask about allergies, or give discharge instructions. This system eliminates that gap entirely — the treatment plan appears in the patient's language, the AI speaks it aloud, and the medic can communicate through translated messaging.

### Volunteers Need Guardrails
In mass casualty events, volunteer responders with basic first aid training are asked to make triage decisions they've never trained for. This system walks them through it step by step, generates the right protocol automatically, and flags when to escalate to a surgeon.
